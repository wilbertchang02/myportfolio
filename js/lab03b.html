Popular Food Dishes from Rio de Janeiro
1. Feijoada

Feijoada is often coined as the Brazilian national dish, is often associated with the colonial population in Rio. It is typically eaten for lunch and is basically consisted of stewed beans in a pork of beef gravy, often accompanied with potatoes, crunchy pork cracklings, kale, fluffy farofa (or toasted cassava flour) and orange slices (which supposedly help with digestion). Depending on where you are in Brazil, the dish can be mixed with various parts of the pig, although the most common tend to be with rib meat and sausages. It also takes up to 24 hours to make, as the beans need to soak for some time in order to achieve the ultimate flavour.

(Source: https://strawberrytours.com/5-can-t-miss-foods-in-rio-de-janeiro)

2. Churrascaria

Churrascaria is basically an all-you-can-eat steakhouse or restaurant, where diners can pick and choose what type of meats to chow down on. The dish was inspired by the “cowboy cuisine” which originated from the natives of Rio Grande do Sul, in the southern parts of Brazil. In restaurants, you can choose between over 100 different hot and cold items from the buffet. The ultimate delicacies-version of Churrascaria however, tends to include lamb chops, Argentine shoulder steak, beef ribs or butterfried top sirloin. Although Churrascaria is becoming more and more popular in America, it is hard to beat the true-blue Churrascaria which can be found in countless places around Rio de Janeiro.

(Source: https://strawberrytours.com/5-can-t-miss-foods-in-rio-de-janeiro)

Culture
Rio de Janiero: Brazil's Cultural Capital

The culture of Rio de Janeiro is permeated by various traditions and popular festivals that reflect the joy and contagious energy of the city. Rio Carnival, for example, is a true explosion of colours, music, and dance, with samba school parades and carnival street blocks that attract crowds through the city streets. During this festivity, it is possible to experience the intensity and passion of the city for music and celebration.
